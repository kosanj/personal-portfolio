Safety-Critical Pacemaker System
Project Duration: September 2023 - December 2023
Course: MECHTRON 3K04 - Software Development

Figure 1 - Simulink overview for the project.

Using a FRDM-K64 board with heart, pacemaker, and accelerometer components on-board, in a team of six we developed a pacemaker model on Simulink for various pacemaker modes (AOO, VOO, AAI, VVI, and their rate-adaptive versions) adhering to the Boston Scientific pacemaker specifications documentation. We built a python GUI as an external DCM to allow for medical parameters to be changed on the DCM, validated and sent to Simulink to update the pacing functionality.

Along with this, we followed the software development processes discussed in class, performed hazard analysis and built a GSN assurance case. This was to emphasize the importance of safety in our design.

7-Segment Display Digital Circuit Optimization
Project Duration: December 2023
Course: MECHTRON 2MD3 - Data Structures & Algorithms

Figure 1 - Physical build for the circuit.

Used digital logic design with various physical components including boolean logic gates and J-K flip flops. Analytically developed a functional circuit, optimized to have the lowest number of physical components (by comparing SOP and POS implementations and simplifying by replacing a flip flop with a boolean expression). The analytical solution was implemented in Multisim (a circuit analysis software) to simulate the results, then built in lab with real circuit components.

"Walk-N-Roll": A 2-in-1 Walker-Wheelchair
Project Duration: March 2022 - April 2022
Course: IBEHS 1P10 - Health Solutions Design Projects I

Figure 1 - Wheelchair configuration of final prototype.

Figure 2 - Walker configuration of final prototype.

In a group of four, we designed a 2-in-1 convertible walker-wheelchair requested by a real stroke survivor with mobility issues. We used bicycle wheels and wood to make the majority of the prototype, and we also used hinges to lock the different configurations, and we also made a cushion that could be stored be stored under the seat when switching the configuration.

GenuCheck: A Knee Rehabilitation Device
Project Duration: January 2022 - March 2022
Course: IBEHS 1P10 - Health Solutions Design Projects I

Figure 1 - Low-level knee brace prototype.

Figure 2 - Sample output for the monitoring visualization feature of the design.

In a group of five, we designed a knee brace with an orientation sensor to detect if a patient's knee had bent too far. Practically, this would be used for rehabilitation and monitoring after knee injuries such as MCL or ACL tears. The logic was implemented using a Raspberry Pi with an orientation sensor, as well as a buzzer to make a sound and vibrate if a maximum angle threshold set by the physician was exceeded. This device would also continuosly keep count of how many times in a day that the maximum angle had been passed, and would update a stored graph with tallies for the week, at the end of each day, or if a pushbutton was pressed on the Raspberry Pi. MagnOstomy
Project Duration: September 2021 - October 2021
Course: IBEHS 1P10 - Health Solutions Design Projects I

Figure 1 - MagnOstomy prototype poster.

In a group of four, we developed a concept and prototype for an ostomy bag configuration which would discreetly light up if the configuration was not secure. Using magnets, electrical wires, and a battery to create a series circuit, we showed how the circuit would light up if the series circuit became disconnected at any of the four corners where magnets were placed.
